# Homepage Blueprint

This document turns the homepage narrative brief into a concrete structure for design and implementation.

---

## Core Direction

Homepage should behave like a:

- cinematic statement
- personal brand experience
- sharp editorial landing page

It should **sell Wahyu and his way of thinking first**, then support that with proof.

It should **not** behave like:

- a standard portfolio homepage
- a service page
- a text-heavy SEO explainer
- a corporate consultant website

---

## Primary Goal

Make visitors:

1. remember the brand
2. understand the point of view
3. trust the technical depth
4. continue into projects / writing / contact

---

## Audience Priority

Homepage should be optimized first for:

1. freelance clients
2. SEO peers
3. marketing managers
4. hiring managers / recruiters

This means the homepage should feel:

- memorable enough for clients
- intelligent enough for SEO peers
- clear enough for business-side readers

---

## Narrative Structure

Use **Option C** as the base sequence:

1. Strong hero
2. Manifesto / belief
3. Authority / writing
4. Work
5. CTA

This should be interpreted as a narrative flow:

1. `Arrival`
2. `Point of view`
3. `Credibility`
4. `Proof`
5. `Conversion`

---

## Section Blueprint

### 1. Hero

#### Job

Create first impression and memorability.

#### Should communicate

- Wahyu is different
- Wahyu is strategic
- Wahyu is credible
- this portfolio is not generic

#### Content style

- very low text density
- one strong statement
- one short support line at most
- CTA can exist, but should not dominate

#### Motion role

- motion can lead here
- hero should be the strongest animated moment on the homepage
- animation should feel intentional, premium, and alive
- should remain SSR-friendly and lightweight

#### Avoid

- long intro paragraph
- too many badges / cards / metrics at once
- generic SEO consultant language

---

### 2. Manifesto / Point Of View

#### Job

Show how Wahyu thinks about SEO.

#### Should communicate

- SEO is not only execution
- SEO is structure, systems, and clarity
- Wahyu has a strong perspective, not just operational skill

#### Content style

- sharp, compact statements
- more like a manifesto or editorial note
- not long explanatory paragraphs

#### Motion role

- scroll-triggered pacing
- typography and reveal timing matter more than decorative animation

#### Avoid

- repeating hero message
- generic value proposition blocks

---

### 3. Authority / Credibility

#### Job

Prevent the homepage from feeling all style and no substance.

#### Trust signals to emphasize

- thinking / method
- SEO depth

#### Optional content formats

- short method block
- concise credibility panel
- selected writing / insight teaser
- one compact “how I think / what I focus on” section

#### Content style

- concise
- intelligent
- structured

#### Avoid

- full CV section on homepage
- too many credentials at once
- over-explaining technical terms

---

### 4. Selected Work

#### Job

Provide proof after the brand and thinking are established.

#### Should communicate

- there is real work behind the point of view
- projects have outcomes
- work is structured and intentional

#### Content style

- few items only
- curated, not exhaustive
- enough detail to trigger interest
- deeper case-study content belongs on the projects page

#### Motion role

- transitions and hover behavior can be expressive
- should support premium feel, not clutter

#### Avoid

- dumping too many projects
- full case-study detail on homepage

---

### 5. Writing / Insight

#### Job

Support authority through ideas and perspective.

#### Should communicate

- Wahyu thinks deeply
- Wahyu documents ideas
- writing is part of the professional brand

#### Content style

- editorial
- selective
- not blog-heavy on homepage

#### Avoid

- a large blog grid
- long article excerpts

---

### 6. CTA

#### Job

Give a clear next step without breaking the tone.

#### CTA direction

- simple
- confident
- calm
- not aggressive or overly salesy

#### Good CTA themes

- start a conversation
- view selected work
- explore writing

---

## Homepage Copy Rules

Homepage copy should be:

- lighter than the current version
- faster to scan
- more statement-led
- less explanatory

Guideline:

- hero: minimal
- manifesto: short
- authority: concise
- work: curated summaries
- writing: teaser only

Detailed explanations should move to:

- projects page
- blog
- experience page

---

## Motion Rules

Motion is allowed to be an attraction.

But it must still feel:

- premium
- intentional
- brand-led
- readable

### Good motion uses

- strong hero entrance
- layered reveal timing
- scroll pacing
- refined hover states
- typography transitions

### Bad motion uses

- random decorative movement
- too many competing animated elements
- motion that reduces clarity
- startup-style microinteraction overload

---

## Visual Strategy

Keep:

- light palette
- neutral tones
- strong typography
- premium minimalism
- editorial rhythm

Push further:

- more distinct hero
- stronger section pacing
- more cinematic tension
- more intentional whitespace

Avoid:

- corporate blocks
- generic feature-card layouts
- overly dense homepage copy

---

## Implementation Implication

The current homepage should be redesigned around this new structure.

### Replace

- category-like cards that explain too much
- standard portfolio-section sequencing
- repeated descriptive copy

### Introduce

- statement-led hero
- manifesto section
- authority section with stronger point of view
- more curated proof section
- lighter writing section

---

## Final Principle

The homepage should answer this sequence:

1. Who is this person?
2. Why does this feel different?
3. How does he think?
4. Can he actually back it up?
5. Do I want to keep exploring?

If a section does not support that sequence, it should be removed, merged, or rewritten.
